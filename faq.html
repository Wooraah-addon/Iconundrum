Frequently asked questions

Everything you might wonder about Iconundrum — the modes, the scores, and playing with friends. Can't find your answer? There's a Suggest a feature / Report a bug button at the bottom of the home screen.

The basics

What is Iconundrum?

A free, fan-made World of Warcraft guessing game. Name an item from its icon, call its auction-house price, or ride a higher-lower chain of values. Play solo, share a challenge link, or gather friends in a live lobby.

Is it free? Do I need an account?

It's completely free — no sign-up, no ads, no tracking. Just pick a name and play. Your stats and best scores live in your own browser.

What are the game modes?

Guess the Item — name the item from its icon, four choices, faster answers score more. Guess the Value — guess an item's gold price; the closer you are, the more you score, and an exact call hits the jackpot. Higher or Lower — call whether the next item is worth more or less, and keep your streak alive.

Does it work on my phone?

Yes — the layout adapts to phones and tablets, so you can play or join a friend's lobby from anywhere.

Scores & leaderboards

What does "ranked" mean?

The global all-time leaderboards only admit games played on the default settings with the Everything category — that's the level playing field everyone competes on. Change any setting (rounds, timer, a themed category, extra lives, and so on) and your game still counts, but on that challenge's own board instead of the global one. Each setup screen shows a live note telling you which you're on, and there's a Reset to defaults button to get back to ranked.

Why didn't my score post to the leaderboard?

A few reasons, and the end-of-game screen always tells you which: you were on custom settings (so it posted to the challenge board only, not the global ranked one); you'd already posted a score on that exact board (replays are for fun — only your first run counts); or it couldn't reach the leaderboards and was saved locally. Same board, one ranked score per player.

What's a challenge link?

Every game has a shareable link that gives everyone who opens it the identical rounds, so you can compete fairly on a shared board — even after the fact. Done with an old one cluttering your home screen? Hit the × to dismiss it.

Playing with friends

How do I play with other people?

Two ways. Share a challenge link and everyone plays the same rounds at their own pace, comparing scores on a shared board. Or open a live lobby for a real-time game night, where you all play together and watch the standings shuffle between rounds.

Someone gave me a game code — where do I put it?

Type it into the join box on the home screen and you'll drop straight into their lobby (or, if it's already started, play it as a challenge).

Can I host games for my stream or community?

Yes — hosting is built with streamers and community game nights in mind. Open a lobby, share the code or link with your viewers, and run a round together. There's a ready-check, host controls, and a streamer-safe sound option to keep things quiet for broadcast.

Sound, data & the small print

What are the sound packs?

Tap the sliders icon by the speaker (top-right) to pick a pack. Classic is the original sounds; Alliance and Horde add faction voice lines to the big moments. There may be a third one, locked for now… and a streamer-safe option that trims the loud stingers.

Where do the items and prices come from?

The pool is built from live EU auction-house data, and each reveal shows real market stats. Prices refresh periodically, so they track what items actually sell for. Item icons and names come straight from the game.

Is this made by Blizzard?

No. Iconundrum is a free, non-commercial fan project. World of Warcraft, item icons and names are © Blizzard Entertainment. It's not affiliated with or endorsed by Blizzard.

I found a bug, or I have an idea!

Brilliant — use the Report a bug or Suggest a feature buttons at the bottom of the home screen. It's a casual side project, but feedback genuinely shapes what gets built next.
