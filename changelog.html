What is Iconundrum?

A free fan-made WoW guessing game: name the item from its icon, call its auction-house price, or ride the higher-lower chain. Play solo, share a challenge link (everyone gets the identical rounds), or gather friends in a live lobby. No sign-up, no ads, no tracking — built for friends, guilds, and community game nights.

Changelog

v0.6.3 — Polish 11 June 2026

  • The home-screen record is clearer: total games and favourite mode on one line, your high scores on default settings on the next — matching what the ranked board counts, so a 20-round marathon doesn't masquerade as your best.
  • A little more life: drifting gold motes behind the home screen, button and card micro-motion, a pulse on the timer's final seconds, reveals that pop. All of it stands down if your system asks for reduced motion.

v0.6.2 — One shot per board 11 June 2026

  • Scores post the first time you play a board — replaying a challenge link is still fun, but a memorized run doesn't go to any leaderboard. The challenge banner tells you when you're revisiting a board you've already played.
  • Leaderboards now show each player once (their posted score), instead of one name filling half the table.
  • The lobby has a Main Menu button — leaving takes you off the roster, and if the host walks, waiting players are told instead of staring at a dead lobby. (In-game, clicking the Iconundrum wordmark up top has always been the way home.)

v0.6.1 — Ranked all-time boards 11 June 2026

  • The all-time leaderboard is now ranked: it only lists games played on each mode's standard ruleset (5 rounds, default timer, standard scoring), so a 20-round marathon can't out-point a 5-round game on volume alone. Any category and either price basis still count — they don't change the score ceiling.
  • Custom-rules games are marked on the results screen and compete on their challenge link's board, exactly as before. Nothing about challenge boards changed.
  • Hard mode is benched hours after launch — ten seconds to type an item name was goblin cruelty, not difficulty. It'll be back once the pacing is fair. Existing hard-mode links keep working.

v0.6 — Ready check 11 June 2026

  • Lobby ready checks — the host pings everyone before launching, the real WoW ready-check chime plays, and ticks and crosses appear in the roster as players answer. Not required to launch — just very WoW.
  • Hard mode for Guess the Icon: no choices — type the item name. Spelling is forgiving (case and punctuation never count, one typo slides on long names), your pick locks into the challenge link, and hard games keep their own leaderboard.
  • Quick-entry buttons on value guesses: tap +1k / +10k / +100k / +1m to build your guess — no fighting the phone keyboard.
  • Your record on the home screen: games played, best score per mode, favourite mode. Stored on your device only — nothing leaves it.
  • Mobile polish: Higher-or-Lower cards now sit compactly side-by-icon on small screens, and the value entry no longer overflows narrow phones.
  • The logo went on a diet (194KB → 73KB) — the home page loads that bit faster.

v0.5.1 — Little things 11 June 2026

  • What's-new strip on the home screen — the latest changelog entry, one click to expand, no trip to the changelog page needed.
  • The post-game "All modes" button now says "Main Menu", because that's what it is.

v0.5 — Pick your price basis 11 June 2026

  • Value and Higher-or-Lower games now let you choose what you're guessing: the sale average (TSM — what items actually sell for, the new default) or the market average (what they're posted for). Your pick shows in setup and in-game, locks into the challenge link, and each basis keeps its own leaderboard. Old links keep working exactly as before.
  • Value guesses take shorthand now — 25k, 1.5m, and 25,000 all work.
  • Reveals tell you how far off you were ("18% low"), and Higher-or-Lower shows the price gap ("2.3× higher").
  • Fixed: a wildly-off value guess was reported as "No guess" — it now gets the "Way off" it deserves.

v0.4 — Synced rounds 11 June 2026

  • In multiplayer Icon and Value games, the host now controls the pace: the "Start next round" button unlocks a few seconds after everyone's timer has run out, and all players flip to the next round together.
  • Rolling standings between rounds — watch the leaderboard shuffle as scores land.
  • If the host wanders off, players can drop to their own pace after a minute.
  • Lobbies that were never launched expire after 20 minutes — their links turn into regular challenge links instead of an eternal waiting room.
  • Higher or Lower lobbies keep the synced start, then it's every goblin for themselves — last streak standing.

v0.3 — Multiplayer lobbies 11 June 2026

  • Create a live lobby from any game's setup screen — friends join via the lobby link and appear in the roster in real time.
  • Host launches when ready; everyone gets a synced 10-second countdown into the identical board.
  • Lobby links keep working after game night as regular challenge links.

v0.2 — Make it yours 11 June 2026

  • Pre-game setup: pick a category (Transmog, Trade Goods, Consumables, Recipes, Mounts & Curios — or Everything), rounds, and timer.
  • Scoring options: speed bonus on/off, value-guess strictness (Casual / Goblin / Tycoon), Higher-or-Lower call tightness.
  • Game codes: copy your challenge link before playing, then compete on the same board.
  • Sounds (with mute toggle) and the proper logo.

v0.1 — Launch 11 June 2026

  • Three modes: Guess the Icon, Guess the Value, Higher or Lower.
  • 600-item pool built from live EU auction-house data, with real market stats on every reveal.
  • Challenge links: one URL, same rounds for everyone, shared leaderboard. Per-challenge and all-time boards.
