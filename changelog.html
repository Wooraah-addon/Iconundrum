What is Iconundrum?

A free fan-made WoW guessing game: name the item from its icon, call its auction-house price, or ride the higher-lower chain. Play solo, share a challenge link (everyone gets the identical rounds), or gather friends in a live lobby. No sign-up, no ads, no tracking — built for friends, guilds, and community game nights.

Changelog

v0.10 — 1,000-item pool June 2026

  • The item pool has grown from 600 to 1,000 items — weapons, armour, mounts, trade goods, consumables and cosmetics from every expansion, all curated and AH-tradeable.
  • Weapons & Armour now make up most of the pool: axes, swords, staves, bows, guns, polearms and iconic gear from Classic through The War Within. Even veteran players will find plenty to humble them.
  • Fresh prices throughout, and old challenge links still play their original item set.

v0.9 — Last Man Standing June 2026

  • A new way to play Higher or Lower with friends. Create a lobby and pick Game style → Last Man Standing: everyone faces the same card at the same time, a wrong call (or running out of time) costs a life, and the last player standing wins. You choose 1–4 lives when you host, and a final placing table crowns the winner — if the last survivors fall together, they go to sudden-death rounds to settle it.
  • Race mode — same chain, your own pace, beat the streak — is still there as the other game style.

v0.8 — Sound packs, a clearer name & Memory Match June 2026

  • A new Memory Match game on the home screen — flip the tiles, find the matching item pairs, and beat your best in Easy, Medium or Hard. A relaxed one: no WoW knowledge needed and no leaderboard, just you against your own best.
  • Faction sound packs — tap the sliders icon by the speaker (top-right) and pick Alliance (human peasant — "Right-o!") or Horde (orc peon — "Work, work."); the big moments get WoW voice lines, while Classic keeps the original sounds. Preview buttons, a streamer-safe option, and a mysterious third pack… locked for now.
  • "Guess the Icon" is now "Guess the Item" — clearer for naming the item from its icon, and it leaves room for a future Guess the Spell. Nothing about play changes; scores and leaderboards carry over.
  • A small × to dismiss an old challenge from your home screen (the link still works if you want it back).
  • A new FAQ page (linked in the footer) covering the modes, what "ranked" means, and how scores post.

v0.7 — Bigger pools, a proper race & a craft pass June 2026

  • Higher or Lower is now a multiplayer race — launch it from a lobby and everyone rides the same chain at their own pace, with live standings and a 💀 when a streak ends. Fall early and you spectate to the finish. The default game is now 10 rounds.
  • A cleaner item pool (v2): every multi-rank crafted item is gone (no more "okay, but which rank?"), ~140 weak entries culled, fresh EU prices throughout — and smarter, wider wrong answers so you can't learn the usual suspects. Old challenge links still play their original pool.
  • The category picker became a grid of icon tiles with flagship faces and clearer names; the near-empty Recipes category retired.
  • Honest leaderboards: ranked now means default rules and the Everything category; the end-of-game screen always tells you whether (and why) your score posted; and there's a Reset to defaults button. Plus a fix for a bug that briefly stopped scores saving.
  • Auctioneer Drezbit heckles your Value guesses — 114 lines, keyed to how far off you were.
  • A craft pass throughout: the Cinzel display typeface, the gold logo in-game, a sharpened home screen, one coherent gold icon set, button feel and depth, score count-ups and little celebrations. All of it respects "reduced motion".

v0.6 — Game night: lobbies, leaderboards & feedback June 2026

  • Ready checks, one-click join by game code, a join box on the home screen, and proper link previews in Discord and Twitch chat.
  • Fairer lobbies: your result stays hidden until the round ends for everyone (no peeking off a friend's screen), hosts can remove players, and it's one entry per device.
  • Livelier standings — the points you scored each round and ▲▼ movement in the rankings — plus a leaderboard refresh so late scores still show up.
  • Ranked all-time boards (default ruleset only) and one ranked shot per board, so a memorized replay can't farm the leaderboard.
  • Extra lives for Higher or Lower, quick-entry value buttons, and a personal-record strip on the home screen.
  • Report a bug / Suggest a feature buttons, keyboard play and accessibility polish, and behind-the-scenes score and lobby hardening.

v0.5 — Price basis & sharing June 2026

  • Choose what you're guessing in Value and Higher or Lower: the sale average (what items actually sell for, the default) or the market average (what they're posted for) — each keeps its own board. Value guesses take shorthand (25k, 1.5m), and reveals tell you how far off you were.
  • A what's-new strip on the home screen, so you can see the latest without leaving.

v0.3–0.4 — Multiplayer foundations June 2026

  • Create a live lobby from any setup screen — friends join via the link and appear in the roster in real time. A synced countdown drops everyone into the identical board, the host paces the rounds, and standings shuffle between them. Lobby links live on afterwards as regular challenge links.

v0.1–0.2 — Launch June 2026

  • Three modes — Guess the Item, Guess the Value, Higher or Lower — on a 600-item pool built from live EU auction-house data, with real market stats on every reveal.
  • Challenge links give everyone the identical rounds and a shared leaderboard (per-challenge and all-time), and pre-game setup lets you pick category, rounds, timer and scoring.
